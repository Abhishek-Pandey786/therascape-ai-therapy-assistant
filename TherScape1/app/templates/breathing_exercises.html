favorite Breathing Exercises

Guided breathing techniques to reduce stress, anxiety, and promote relaxation

Breathing exercises are powerful tools that can help calm your mind, reduce stress, and bring you back to the present moment. When practiced regularly, they can help manage anxiety, improve focus, and promote overall well-being.

Try these different breathing techniques and find the ones that work best for you.

4-7-8 Breathing

This relaxing breath practice is often recommended to help with anxiety, stress, and even falling asleep. It acts as a natural tranquilizer for the nervous system.

How to Practice 4-7-8 Breathing

  1. Find a comfortable position sitting or lying down.
  2. Place the tip of your tongue against the ridge behind your upper front teeth and keep it there throughout the exercise.
  3. Exhale completely through your mouth, making a whoosh sound.
  4. Close your mouth and inhale quietly through your nose for a count of 4.
  5. Hold your breath for a count of 7.
  6. Exhale completely through your mouth, making a whoosh sound, for a count of 8.
  7. Repeat this cycle 3-4 times.

Box Breathing

Also known as square breathing, this technique is used by everyone from athletes to Navy SEALs to help improve concentration and performance while decreasing stress.

How to Practice Box Breathing

  1. Sit upright in a comfortable position with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Slowly exhale all of the air from your lungs.
  3. Inhale slowly through your nose to a count of 4, filling your lungs.
  4. Hold your breath for a count of 4.
  5. Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of 4.
  6. Hold your breath (lungs empty) for a count of 4.
  7. Repeat for at least 5-10 minutes to experience the full benefits.

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Also called belly breathing or deep breathing, this technique helps you use your diaphragm properly while breathing to strengthen it, decrease oxygen demand, and slow your breathing rate.

How to Practice Diaphragmatic Breathing

  1. Lie on your back with your knees slightly bent and your head on a pillow.
  2. Place one hand on your upper chest and the other on your belly, just below your rib cage.
  3. Breathe in slowly through your nose, feeling your stomach push against your hand. The hand on your chest should remain as still as possible.
  4. Tighten your stomach muscles and let them fall inward as you exhale through pursed lips. The hand on your chest should remain still.
  5. Repeat for 5-10 minutes, practicing 3-4 times per day.

lightbulb Tip

Once you've mastered this technique lying down, you can practice it while sitting or standing.

Alternate Nostril Breathing

This yogic breathing technique (Nadi Shodhana) helps balance the two hemispheres of the brain, promoting mental clarity and alleviating stress.

How to Practice Alternate Nostril Breathing

  1. Sit comfortably with your back straight and shoulders relaxed.
  2. Place your left hand on your left knee with palm facing upward.
  3. Lift your right hand to your nose. Use your right thumb to close your right nostril and your right ring finger to close your left nostril.
  4. Close your right nostril with your thumb and inhale slowly through your left nostril.
  5. At the peak of your inhalation, close your left nostril with your ring finger, release your thumb from your right nostril, and exhale slowly through your right nostril.
  6. Inhale through your right nostril, then close it with your thumb, release your ring finger from your left nostril, and exhale through your left nostril.
  7. This completes one cycle. Continue for 5-10 cycles.

lightbulb Tip

Keep your breathing smooth and even. If you feel dizzy or lightheaded at any point, return to normal breathing.

Breath Counting

A simple mindfulness practice that helps calm the mind and improve concentration.

How to Practice Breath Counting

  1. Sit in a comfortable position with your back straight but not stiff.
  2. Close your eyes or maintain a soft gaze.
  3. Take a few deep breaths, then allow your breathing to return to its natural rhythm.
  4. Count each breath as you exhale, starting from 1.
  5. Continue counting up to 10, then start over from 1.
  6. If your mind wanders, gently bring your attention back to your breath and start counting from 1 again.
  7. Practice for 5-10 minutes daily.

lightbulb Tip

Don't worry if you lose count or get distracted. Simply return to counting from 1. The goal is to focus on your breath, not to reach a specific number.
