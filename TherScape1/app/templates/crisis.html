phone Crisis Helplines

Emergency contacts and resources for immediate support in India

warning

If you are in immediate danger or having thoughts of harming yourself or others

Call emergency services (112 in India) or go to your nearest emergency room.

The following helplines and resources are available to provide support during a crisis. All services are confidential and many offer support via phone, text, or chat.

National Crisis Helplines

NIMHANS Mental Health Helpline

phone Call: 080-46110007
language Website: nimhans.ac.in

The National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS) provides 24/7, free and confidential support for people in distress, prevention and crisis resources.

iCall Helpline

phone Call: 022-25521111 / +91-9152987821
email Email: icall@tiss.edu
language Website: icallhelpline.org

iCall is a psychosocial helpline that offers counseling via telephone, email and chat to individuals in emotional distress. Available Monday to Saturday, 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

Kiran Mental Health Helpline

phone Call: 1800-599-0019 (Toll-Free)

A 24/7 toll-free mental health rehabilitation helpline launched by the Social Justice and Empowerment Ministry, Government of India. It provides support in 13 languages.

Specialized Crisis Support

Vandrevala Foundation

phone Call: +91-9999666555
email Email: help@vandrevalafoundation.com
language Website: vandrevalafoundation.com

The Vandrevala Foundation provides 24/7 free counseling services and mental health resources to those who are in emotional distress and their family members.

AASRA

phone Call: +91-9820466726
email Email: aasrahelpline@yahoo.com
language Website: aasra.info

AASRA is a crisis intervention center for the lonely, distressed, and suicidal. Available 24/7.

Sneha India Foundation

phone Call: 044-24640050 / +91-9566067080
email Email: help@snehaindia.org
language Website: snehaindia.org

Sneha provides 24/7 crisis intervention and emotional support for people in distress. They offer support in multiple languages.

security Safety Information

  • If you're having thoughts of suicide, please reach out to one of the helplines above immediately.
  • Remove any dangerous objects from your environment.
  • Stay connected with trusted friends or family members.
  • Consider going to your nearest emergency room if you're in immediate danger.
  • Remember that these feelings are temporary and help is available.
